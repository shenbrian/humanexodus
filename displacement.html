What I've seen in the data

People move. Always.

I know that sounds like something someone says to make you feel better. I'm not trying to do that. I'm trying to show you the actual pattern.

Every wave of displacement — tech, manufacturing, finance, doesn't matter — looks at the moment it happens like an ending. The data shows it isn't. People reposition. Not all at once, not without pain, but they move. I've been building a system to track exactly how.

01 —

What you say you'll do and what you do are different things

That's not a criticism — it's just true for everyone. The gap between intention and action is where the interesting stuff lives. We track both, 30 days apart.

02 —

The headlines are lying about the timeline

Disruption moves slower than the articles say and faster than you think you have time for. You probably have more runway than you feel right now.

03 —

Sideways beats starting over

The people I've tracked who land well mostly go sideways, not back to zero. Same domain, shifted function. Your existing knowledge isn't the liability — it's the asset.

04 —

The first 90 days matter more than the decision

Not because you need a plan. Because small moves in the first 90 days tend to compound. In either direction. Just keep moving.
